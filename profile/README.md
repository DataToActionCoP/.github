Global Public Health Data Science Community (GPH-CoP)

Welcome to the Global Public Health Data Science Community of Practice (CoP). This initiative bridges the gap in cross-border health data collaboration, providing a unified GitHub environment for public health data scientists across the US, EU, East Asia, and Southeast Asia.

🔭 Our Vision

To establish an international community where health experts collaborate on public datasets, analytic code, and AI/ML models. We aim to accelerate innovations in disease surveillance and modeling while ensuring strict adherence to regional regulations (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.) and ethical norms.

🎯 Strategic Objectives

Shared Repository Ecosystem: Hosting standardized public datasets, synthetic data, and metadata.

Collaborative AI/ML: Developing models for outbreak detection, forecasting, and resource allocation.

Best Practice Guidelines: Disseminating standards for data anonymization, FAIR compliance, and "Model Passports."

Capacity Building: Organizing cross-regional hackathons, mentorship, and training modules.

Policy Influence: Engaging with global regulators (CDC, ECDC, ASEAN, WHO) to align community practices with evolving standards.

🏗 Technical Architecture

The organization is structured to facilitate both global collaboration and regional sovereignty:

⚖️ Governance & Ethics

The CoP is guided by a 12-person Steering Committee and five specialized working groups:

Data Stewardship & Ethics: Legal checklists and anonymization guidelines.

Technical Infrastructure: Repository architecture and CI/CD pipelines.

Modelling & Analytics: Model transparency and performance evaluation.

Capacity Building: Mentorship and localization of training materials.

Communications: Community outreach and newsletter management.

🚀 Get Involved

We welcome participation from public health agencies, universities, NGOs, and individual researchers.

Explore: Check our Wiki for the Community Charter and Code of Conduct.

Contribute: Visit the datasets or models repositories to see how to submit your work.

Discuss: Join our GitHub Discussions to meet other members.

Attend: Stay tuned for our annual virtual hackathon and rotating in-person meetings.

Note on Data Privacy: Contributors must commit to sharing only public, anonymized, or synthetic data. All collaborative work is conducted under the community's established Data Sharing Policy.
